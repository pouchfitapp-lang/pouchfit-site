1. Overview

PouchFit ("we," "our," "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and safeguard your information when you use the PouchFit mobile application (the "App" or "Service").

By creating an account or using PouchFit, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree with any part of this Privacy Policy, please do not use the App.

This Privacy Policy should be read alongside our Terms of Use.

2. Information We Collect

PouchFit collects the minimum amount of data necessary to provide and improve our Service.

2.1 Account Information (stored on our servers)

PouchFit does not store passwords. We use third-party authentication providers — Sign in with Apple and Google Sign-In — to verify your identity. When you sign up, the provider shares limited account information with us:

  • Your name
  • Your email address (or an Apple private relay alias, if you choose "Hide My Email" during Apple sign-up)
  • A unique user identifier from the provider, used to recognize you on subsequent sign-ins

This information is stored securely on our servers. For full details about what each provider shares, how Apple's "Hide My Email" works, and how to revoke access, see Authentication & Sign-In Data  →.

2.2 Exercise & Workout Data (stored locally on your device)

  • Exercise history and session logs
  • Daily and weekly progress data
  • Streak and goal completion records
  • Exercise preferences and settings

This data lives exclusively on your device and is never transmitted to our servers.

2.3 Screen Time Data (processed on-device only)

PouchFit uses Apple's Screen Time API (FamilyControls framework) to manage app blocking. All Screen Time data — including which apps you select for blocking — is processed entirely on your device. We never access, collect, transmit, or store this data on any external server.

2.4 Subscription Information (managed by Apple)

Subscription purchases, billing, and payment information are managed entirely by Apple through StoreKit. We receive only your subscription status (active, expired, etc.) to unlock premium features. We do not process, store, or have access to your payment details, credit card information, or Apple ID.

2.5 Automatically Collected Information

We do not currently use third-party analytics, crash reporting, or advertising SDKs. If we introduce analytics or diagnostics tools in the future, we will update this Privacy Policy before doing so.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Provide the Service — track your exercise progress, manage app blocking based on your Screen Time preferences, display Live Activity updates during workout sessions, and manage your subscription status.
  • Account management — maintain your account, authenticate your identity, and allow you to access PouchFit across devices.
  • Communications — send service-related messages such as account confirmations, security notices, and important updates about the App.
  • Marketing (with your consent) — if you opt in during sign-up, we may send promotional emails including tips, product updates, feature announcements, and special offers. You can opt out at any time (see Section 8).
  • Improve the Service — use aggregated, anonymized data to understand usage patterns and improve PouchFit.

We will never sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties.

Authentication & Sign-In Data

This section explains how Apple and Google sign-in work in PouchFit, and exactly what we receive from each provider. It is incorporated by reference into the Privacy Policy above.

How sign-in works in PouchFit

PouchFit uses two third-party authentication providers: Sign in with Apple and Google Sign-In. We do not store passwords ourselves — authentication is handled entirely by Apple or Google. When you sign in, the provider verifies your identity and shares a limited set of information with us.

You can sign in with either provider. We do not require you to use both, and we do not link accounts across providers.

What we receive from Apple

When you tap Sign in with Apple, Apple shares the following with us:

  • A unique, stable user identifier — this lets us recognize you across sign-ins. It is unique to PouchFit and cannot be used to identify you in any other app.
  • An email address — either your real Apple ID email, or an anonymized @privaterelay.appleid.com address if you choose "Hide My Email" during sign-up.
  • Your name — only on the first sign-up. Apple does not share your name on subsequent sign-ins, so we save it the first time you authenticate and reuse it from there.

What we receive from Google

When you tap Sign in with Google, Google shares the following with us:

  • A unique Google user ID for your PouchFit account
  • Your email address — your real Gmail or Google Workspace email. Google does not offer an equivalent to Apple's Hide My Email.
  • Your name and profile picture

We request only the basic "email" and "profile" scopes. We do not access your contacts, calendar, Drive files, or any other Google data.

What we store on our servers

From both providers, we store only:

  • Your name
  • Your email address (or Apple relay alias)
  • A reference ID linking you to your authentication provider

We do not store passwords, profile pictures, or any other data the providers may share. Authentication tokens issued by Apple or Google are used only to verify your identity during a session and are not retained long-term.

Apple's "Hide My Email" feature

If you used "Hide My Email" during Apple sign-up, the email address we have on file is an Apple-controlled relay alias that looks like x7y9k2@privaterelay.appleid.com. This alias is unique to PouchFit.

Emails we send to this address — including transactional emails (security notices, subscription confirmations) and marketing emails (if you opted in) — are forwarded by Apple to your real inbox. We never see your real Apple ID email.

You can revoke the email forwarding at any time in your Apple ID settings. Doing so will permanently break our ability to email you, but will not delete your PouchFit account.

Revoking access

You can disconnect PouchFit from your Apple or Google account at any time, directly from the provider's settings:

Apple users

Settings → [Your Name] → Sign in with Apple → PouchFit → Stop Using Apple ID

Google users

Go to myaccount.google.com/permissions → find PouchFit → Remove access

Revoking access at the provider level prevents future sign-ins but does not automatically delete your PouchFit account or the data we have on our servers. To fully delete your account, follow the steps in Section 11 of our Privacy Policy.

Account deletion

When you delete your PouchFit account (via the in-app setting or by emailing hello@pouchfitapp.com), we permanently remove from our servers within 30 days:

  • Your name and email address (or Apple relay alias)
  • The provider reference ID
  • Any other account data stored on our servers

We do not retain authentication tokens or credentials after deletion. If you later sign in again with the same Apple ID or Google account, a fresh PouchFit account will be created.
