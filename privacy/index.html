1. Overview

PouchFit ("we," "our," "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what data PouchFit collects, how we use it, how it's stored, and what rights you have.

This policy mirrors the App Privacy disclosures we file with Apple for our App Store privacy label. If anything in this policy conflicts with our App Store privacy label, the App Store label is authoritative for what is collected.

By creating an account or using PouchFit, you agree to this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the app.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the data we collect to:

  • Provide PouchFit — display your progress, manage your subscription, and enable Live Activities during workout sessions.
  • Account management — authenticate you when you sign in, and recognize you across devices.
  • Service communications — send account confirmations, security notices, and important app updates.
  • Marketing (with your consent) — if you opt in during sign-up, send occasional emails about new features, exercise tips, and offers. You can opt out anytime.
  • Improve PouchFit — analyze aggregated, anonymized patterns of how the app is used.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data.

5. Third-Party Services

PouchFit uses the following third-party services:

If we add other third-party services in the future, we will update this policy and our App Store privacy label before doing so.

6. App Tracking & Advertising

PouchFit does not track you across other apps or websites. We do not participate in ad networks, we have no ad SDKs, and we declare "No" to tracking for every data type in our App Store privacy label.

The Google Sign-In SDK declares "Analytics" use for some of its own internal data (see Section 2.2). This refers to Google's own service analytics, not cross-app advertising tracking. It is not considered tracking under Apple's App Tracking Transparency framework.

Authentication & Sign-In Data

This section explains how Apple and Google sign-in work in PouchFit, and exactly what each provider shares with us. It is incorporated by reference into the Privacy Policy above.

How sign-in works

PouchFit uses two third-party authentication providers: Sign in with Apple and Google Sign-In. We do not store passwords. Authentication is handled entirely by Apple or Google. You can sign in with either provider; we do not require both and we do not link accounts across providers.

What we receive from Apple

Sign in with Apple is a native iOS framework (no third-party SDK). When you tap Sign in with Apple, Apple shares with us:

  • A unique, stable user identifier — lets us recognize you across sign-ins. Unique to PouchFit, not usable to identify you in other apps.
  • An email address — your real Apple ID email, or an anonymized @privaterelay.appleid.com address if you chose "Hide My Email" during sign-up.
  • Your name — only on the first sign-up. Apple does not share your name on subsequent sign-ins, so we save it the first time and reuse it from there.

What we receive from Google

When you tap Sign in with Google, PouchFit directly receives:

  • A unique Google user ID
  • Your email address — your real Gmail or Google Workspace email. Google does not offer an equivalent to Apple's Hide My Email.
  • Your name

We request only the basic email and profile scopes. We do not access your contacts, calendar, Drive files, photos, or any other Google data.

The Google Sign-In SDK itself collects additional data internally per Google's own privacy practices, as disclosed in Section 2.2. That data is handled by Google, not by PouchFit.

What we store on our servers

From both providers, we store only:

  • Your name
  • Your email address (or Apple relay alias)
  • A reference ID linking you to your authentication provider

We do not store passwords, profile pictures, or authentication tokens long-term. Tokens issued by Apple or Google are used only to verify your identity during a session.

Apple's "Hide My Email" feature

If you used "Hide My Email" during Apple sign-up, the email address we have on file is an Apple-controlled relay alias that looks like x7y9k2@privaterelay.appleid.com. This alias is unique to PouchFit.

Emails we send to this address — including transactional emails (security notices, subscription confirmations) and marketing emails (if you opted in) — are forwarded by Apple to your real inbox. We never see your real Apple ID email.

You can revoke email forwarding at any time in your Apple ID settings. Doing so permanently breaks our ability to email you but does not delete your PouchFit account.

Revoking access

You can disconnect PouchFit from your Apple or Google account at any time directly from the provider's settings:

  • Apple users — Settings → [Your Name] → Sign in with Apple → PouchFit → Stop Using Apple ID
  • Google users — Go to myaccount.google.com/permissions → find PouchFit → Remove access

Revoking access at the provider level prevents future sign-ins but does not automatically delete your PouchFit account or the data we have on our servers. To fully delete your account, follow the steps in Section 11.

Account deletion

When you delete your PouchFit account, we permanently remove from our servers within 30 days:

  • Your name and email address (or Apple relay alias)
  • The provider reference ID
  • Any other account data stored on our servers

We do not retain authentication tokens or credentials after deletion. If you later sign in again with the same Apple ID or Google account, a fresh PouchFit account will be created.
